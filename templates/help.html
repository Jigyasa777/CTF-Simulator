Help & FAQs

Find answers to common questions below.

What is the MITRE eCTF?

The MITRE Embedded Capture the Flag (eCTF) is an embedded security competition hosted by MITRE that challenges teams to design secure devices and later attack each other's systems. It's a hands-on, real-world way to learn about embedded cybersecurity.

How do I use the UART simulator?

The Toolkit page includes a UART Simulator tool. Simply type a command into the input box, click the run button, and view the simulated output. It's designed to mimic interaction with a device's serial console.

How do I decode Base64 or use XOR?

On the Toolkit page, select the appropriate option (Base64 or XOR). Enter the encoded text and any required key (for XOR), then press the convert button to see the result.

Who is this dashboard for?

This dashboard is built specifically for ETSU students involved in the MITRE eCTF. It centralizes competition timelines, tools, and learning resources to help you succeed.

Who do I contact for help at ETSU?

If you need assistance, please contact your course instructor or the ETSU cybersecurity department. More support details may be added as the competition progresses.
